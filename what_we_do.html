1 Step 1 Voice | November 19th, 2013

Inside the University Center

1 Step 1 Voice is a national campaign hosted by IJM chapters, uniting supporters and advocates from across the country to let our elected representatives know ending modern slavery must be one of our government’s top priorities. CMU IJM tabled and people to contact their state reps in support of the Human Trafficking Prioritization Act, an important piece of legislation in elevating human trafficking as a foreign policy priority. Through our collective efforts, this national day of action resulted in over 4500 calls to Congress.

Documentary Screening: Born into Brothels | November 12th, 2013

University Center Wright

CMU International Justice Mission hosted a screening of the film Born Into Brothels, a "portrait of several unforgettable children who live in the red light district of Calcutta, where their mothers work as prostitutes." New York-based photographer, Zana Briski, teaches these children to use cameras and how to capture their lives through photography. The film was the winner of the 77th annual Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature. We followed the event with discussion groups.

Project To End Human Trafficking Speaker | October 14, 2013

Porter Hall A19C

To support the internal education of our chapter, we invited Lynsie Cott from The Project To End Human Trafficking to speak about human trafficking basics, such as why human trafficking is so prevalent, how people are trafficked, and how to detect signs of trafficked victims. The Project To End Human Trafficking is a Pittsburgh-based organization, raising awareness of human trafficking and human trafficking prevention through speaking engagements and training sessions.
